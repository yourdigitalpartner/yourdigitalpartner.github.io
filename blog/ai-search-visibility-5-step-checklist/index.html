5 steps to earn AI citations

AI Search Visibility — Your Five Step Checklist

Buyers increasingly ask ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and Google's AI Overview for recommendations before they ever visit a website. If those systems can't clearly read, trust and cite your business, you're invisible at the exact moment a decision is forming.

Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO) fixes that. These five steps, roughly in order of impact, are the foundations that get your business surfaced as the answer, not just ranked in a list of links.

📖 Companion checklist to our guide: 5 Steps to Increase Your AI Search Visibility
1
Answer questions directly to earn AI citations
Can AI answer engines lift a clear answer from your page?

Answer engines extract short, self-contained passages, not whole pages. Open each key section with a plain, direct answer to its question in the first 40-60 words, then add the detail underneath.

2
Use question-style headings AI search can read
Do your headings match how people actually ask AI?

Question-style headings ("What is...", "How much does...") map onto real queries and help models find the right section. Make each section stand on its own, since passages are often extracted in isolation.

3
Add schema markup so AI can trust your facts
Can AI read your key facts as structured data?

Schema (JSON-LD) hands AI systems explicit facts instead of making them infer from prose. Mark up your Organization, FAQPage and HowTo content, matched exactly to what's visible on the page.

4
Keep facts consistent to protect your AI visibility
Does every source give AI the same story about you?

Models build a picture of you from your site, Google Business Profile, LinkedIn and directories at once. When those disagree on your description, pricing or claims, confidence drops, and so does the citation.

5
Earn genuine mentions to build AI visibility
Does the wider web vouch for you to AI?

AI weighs the whole web's view of you, not just your own domain. Genuine mentions from press, directories, reviews and guest content compound over time; mass, low-quality link building does the opposite.
