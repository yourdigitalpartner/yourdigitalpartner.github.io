B2B paid campaigns: questions answered.

Straight answers on Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads and building pipeline from paid media.

How much should a B2B company spend on Google Ads or LinkedIn Ads?

There is no single figure - the right budget depends on your average deal value, sales cycle, target accounts and the cost of the keywords or audiences you are competing for. A more useful approach is to start with enough budget to gather meaningful data on the highest-intent terms and audiences, measure cost per qualified lead and pipeline, then scale what proves it converts. Spend should be judged against pipeline and revenue, not clicks or impressions.

Google Ads or LinkedIn Ads - which is better for B2B?

They do different jobs and work best together. Google Ads captures active demand - people already searching for a solution - so it is strong for high-intent, bottom-of-funnel terms. LinkedIn excels at reaching specific roles, seniorities and accounts to build awareness and demand where search volume is low. A full-funnel programme uses LinkedIn to create and nurture demand and Google to capture it.

How do you measure the success of a B2B paid campaign?

By tracking down-funnel outcomes, not vanity metrics. That means connecting ad spend to qualified leads, opportunities, pipeline and revenue - typically through proper conversion tracking, CRM integration and lead-quality feedback. A campaign with fewer, more expensive clicks that generates real pipeline outperforms one with cheap clicks that never convert.

Why are my B2B paid ads not generating leads?

Common reasons include targeting that is too broad, bidding on low-intent keywords, weak or generic ad creative, landing pages that do not match the ad's promise or convert poorly, no full-funnel structure, and optimising towards clicks instead of lead quality. Fixing targeting, message-match and the landing page experience usually has more impact than increasing budget.

What is full-funnel paid media?

Full-funnel paid media aligns ad formats and messaging to each buying stage - awareness, consideration and decision - rather than pushing a single 'book a demo' ad to everyone. It uses top-of-funnel content to build demand, mid-funnel nurture and retargeting to stay present during evaluation, and high-intent search and lead-gen formats to capture buyers ready to act.
