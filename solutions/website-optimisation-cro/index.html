Improving website conversion: questions answered.

How to turn more of your existing traffic into qualified leads - the questions B2B teams ask most.

How do I improve my website's conversion rate?

Start by understanding where visitors drop off, then remove the friction. Combine analytics, heatmaps and session recordings to see real behaviour, form clear hypotheses, and validate changes with A/B testing rather than guesswork. Typical wins come from clearer messaging and value propositions, stronger and better-placed calls to action, faster and simpler forms, reduced page-load friction, and persona-based content that matches visitor intent.

What is a good conversion rate for a B2B website?

It varies by industry, traffic source and how you define a conversion, but many B2B sites convert visitors to enquiries in the low single-digit percentages. Rather than chasing a benchmark, the more useful question is whether your rate is improving over time and how much pipeline each percentage point represents. Small conversion gains on existing traffic often outperform buying more traffic.

What causes low website conversion?

Common causes include unclear or generic messaging, weak or hidden calls to action, long or complicated forms, slow-loading pages, a poor mobile experience, a mismatch between ad or search intent and the landing page, and a lack of trust signals such as proof, results or credibility. Behaviour data usually reveals which of these is costing you the most enquiries.

What is conversion rate optimisation (CRO)?

Conversion rate optimisation is the structured process of increasing the percentage of visitors who take a desired action - such as submitting an enquiry or booking a call - without necessarily increasing traffic. It combines quantitative data, qualitative behaviour analysis, hypothesis-led testing and iterative improvement to turn more of your existing audience into leads.

How long does CRO take to show results?

Some fixes - clearer CTAs, form improvements or removing obvious friction - can lift results quickly. Robust A/B tests need enough traffic and time to reach statistical confidence, often a few weeks per test. CRO is an ongoing programme of compounding improvements rather than a one-off project, which is why it keeps paying back on traffic you already have.

Do I need more traffic or better conversion?

If you already have meaningful traffic that isn't converting, improving conversion is usually the faster and more cost-effective lever - it increases leads from budget you have already spent. More traffic only helps if the pages it lands on convert. In practice the two work together, which is why conversion optimisation and visibility are best planned side by side.
